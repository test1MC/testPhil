Critical Case Meetings

The purpose of critical case meetings/briefings is to discuss the case background, present status, prognosis, and supervision plans with regional management. The assigned examiner(s) will prepare and present critical cases at the briefings. The Regional Director will determine the format of the critical case package prior to the meeting and communicate the format to examiners a minimum of ten business days in advance of the meeting. The examiner’s supervisor will review the package(s) prior to submission to the regional office for quality and completeness as well as adherence to established standards.
